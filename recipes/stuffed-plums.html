Stuffed plums

Back to Home

Description

Picture yourself inside a woodern house, a great fireplace and the smell of sweet plums drifting through the air. Whilst the housing is not provided, the recipe is more than enough to make up for it

Ingredients

For the dumplings:

  • 1 kg potatoes
  • 2 chicken eggs
  • 500 g flour (+/- 50 g)
  • 2 tablespoons semolina
  • 1 cube of butter
  • 1 pinch of nutmeg
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 15-20 plums

For coating:

  • 200 g ground walnuts
  • 3 tablespoons breadcrumbs
  • Ground cinnamon
  • 100 g brown sugar

Additional:

  • Water + 1 pinch of salt, for boiling

Steps

  1. Clean and wash the potatoes and put them to boil. You can wash them and boil them with the skin on. I clean them and steam them for 20 minutes.
  2. After they are cooked, mash them with a fork, add the butter, semolina, and beaten eggs, and mix quickly until homogeneous. Add the powdered sugar mixed with nutmeg, and the flour. Mix well, then let the dough cool.
  3. Wash and cut the plums in half. If they are small, you can remove the pits and use them whole.
  4. Toast the walnuts a bit and grind them finely. In a bowl, mix the ground walnuts, breadcrumbs, cinnamon, and brown sugar.
  5. Use a well-floured, well-floured surface. Form balls from the dough, press a whole plum or half in the middle, and form a compact ball. Set them aside on a wooden cutting board.
  6. Put a large pot on the stove with water and a pinch of salt. Let it boil and add 5-6 dumplings to the water and let them cook. When they rise to the surface, let them boil for another 4-5 minutes, then remove them with a slotted spoon.
  7. Immediately roll them in the walnut mixture. Arrange them on a platter and keep at room temperature until serving.
